First things first
Getting Started
You'll receive an invite link that takes you to a Set Password page. After that, you're in. Here's what to do first.
1

Set your password

Click the invite link, choose a password, and you'll land on the main dashboard. You have admin access — full control over your tenant's data.

2

Explore the Overview

Your first screen shows KPIs at a glance — utilization rates, bench count, upcoming rolloffs, and demand coverage. Click any card to drill down into the underlying data.

3

Open the Resource Allocation tab

This is the heatmap — a 12-week view of every consultant's hours by project. Click any cell to edit hours inline. Changes save automatically.

4

Check Open Needs

See what roles are needed across projects. The donut chart groups needs by client. Click "Find Matches" on any need to see AI-powered consultant recommendations ranked by skill and availability.

5

Ask Claude anything

Natural language Q&A over your staffing data. Try: "Who's on the bench next month?" or "Which projects are understaffed?" — Claude reads your live data and answers in plain English.

Pro tip: Press Ctrl+K anywhere to open the command palette — search for consultants, skills, projects, or type > for quick actions.

The App
Five Tabs, One Dashboard
Each tab serves a distinct purpose in the staffing workflow.
📊
Overview
Ctrl+1
Your command center. Utilization gauge shows team-wide allocation. Bench list flags who's available. Rolloff timeline shows consultants finishing engagements soon. Demand coverage tracks how well open needs are being filled. Click any KPI card to drill into the underlying detail.
🗓️
Resource Allocation
Ctrl+2
The heatmap — a 12-week grid of consultant hours. Each row is a person, each column is a week, cells show hours by project. Click any cell to edit inline. Color coding: green = healthy utilization, red = overbooked, gray = unassigned. Expand a row to see project-level breakdowns. Use Quick Fill to bulk-assign hours across a date range.
📋
Open Needs
Ctrl+3
Demand pipeline. Each need is a role to fill — level, skill requirements, hours per week, date range. The donut chart groups needs by client. Expand a need to see details. Find Matches triggers the AI recommendations engine — it scores consultants on skill overlap and availability, with green/yellow/coral badges. Accept Match books the hours automatically.
🤖
Ask Claude
Ctrl+4
Natural language interface to your staffing data. Ask questions like "Who has SuiteScript experience and is available in May?" or "Show me all Senior Managers across projects." Claude reads your consultants, assignments, needs, and skills in real time and responds conversationally. Suggested questions refresh based on your current data.
⚙️
Settings
Ctrl+5
Two panels: Consultants (add, edit profiles, manage skills, deactivate) and Users (invite teammates, assign roles, manage access). As an admin, you can invite others to your tenant and control who sees what.

Help us improve
How to Submit Feedback
We've built a structured testing portal right into the app so you can log feedback as you go.
1

Open the Testing Portal

Click "Testing Portal" in the sidebar. It opens in a new tab so you can keep the main app open side by side.

2

Work through the test cases

Each test case has numbered steps and expected results. Follow the steps in the main app, then come back to the portal to mark Pass or Fail. Failed tests ask for a short note on what went wrong.

3

Submit as you go

You don't have to finish everything in one sitting. Your progress saves as you submit each category. Come back anytime to pick up where you left off.

4

Share general feedback

At the bottom of the portal, there's a free-form feedback box. Use it for anything — feature ideas, UX observations, things that surprised you. Every submission is preserved, so send as many as you'd like.

Pro tip: Open the app and the Testing Portal in two browser windows side by side. Follow the test steps in one window, record your results in the other.
